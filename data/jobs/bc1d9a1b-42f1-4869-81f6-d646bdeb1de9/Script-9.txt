DARK COMEDY SCRIPT
Title: “SORRY FOR YOUR LOSS (IT’S COMPLICATED)”

Genre: Dark Comedy / Satire
Estimated Duration: ~45–50 minutes

FADE IN:
EXT. CREMATORIUM PARKING LOT – MORNING

[Approx. Timestamp: 00:00:00]

Grey sky. Drizzle. A banner flaps loosely:

“WELCOME – TODAY’S FUNERAL”

Someone taped over “FUNERAL” with “CELEBRATION OF LIFE,” but it’s peeling.

A hearse stalls. Engine coughs. Silence.

INT. CREMATORIUM – WAITING HALL

[00:02:30]

Rows of plastic chairs. Awkward silence.

Meet the mourners:

DEV (34) – The dead man’s son, emotionally numb

MAYA (32) – Dev’s ex-wife, overly polite

UNCLE GIRISH (55) – Loud, inappropriate

RITA (40) – Funeral coordinator who has seen too much

A phone rings. Everyone stares.

Dev answers.

DEV
Yes. He’s still dead.

Hangs up.

CUT TO:
INT. WAITING HALL – CONTINUOUS

[00:05:30]

Rita checks her clipboard.

RITA
Just confirming… this is a sad funeral, right?

DEV
Define sad.

RITA
Okay. No balloons.

She removes balloons awkwardly.

CUT TO:
EXT. CREMATORIUM BACK AREA

[00:08:00]

Uncle Girish smokes beside the coffin.

GIRISH
(to coffin)
You always said you’d leave early.

Ash falls on the coffin.

GIRISH (CONT’D)
Sorry. Wind.

CUT TO:
INT. HALL – EULOGY SETUP

[00:11:00]

Maya approaches Dev.

MAYA
I’m sorry. For everything.

DEV
You picked a bad day to mean that.

They stand in silence.

Someone accidentally starts HAPPY BIRTHDAY on the speaker.

Rita PANICS.

CUT TO:
INT. CEREMONY HALL – FUNERAL STARTS

[00:14:30]

Dim lights. Heavy silence.

Priest clears throat.

PRIEST
We are gathered to honor—

Microphone SCREECHES.

PRIEST (CONT’D)
—yes, that’s loud.

Everyone winces.

CUT TO:
INT. CEREMONY HALL – EULOGIES

[00:17:30]

Uncle Girish takes the mic.

GIRISH
He was a good man. Not great. But good enough.

Dev stares.

GIRISH (CONT’D)
He borrowed money… and never returned it.

A beat.

GIRISH
Death is closure.

Scattered, uncomfortable laughter.

CUT TO:
INT. HALLWAY – BREAK

[00:20:30]

Dev leans against the wall.

Rita joins him.

RITA
I’ve done this for fifteen years.

DEV
Does it get easier?

RITA
No. It just gets funnier in a way you feel guilty about.

They share a small smile.

CUT TO:
INT. CREMATION ROOM – PREP

[00:24:00]

The coffin is pushed in.

Machine BEEPS.

TECHNICIAN
Power cut.

Silence.

TECHNICIAN (CONT’D)
We’ll resume when electricity comes back.

Everyone just… waits.

CUT TO:
INT. CREMATION ROOM – DARK

[00:27:30]

Emergency lights flicker red.

Uncle Girish whispers.

GIRISH
This is symbolic.

DEV
Please stop talking.

CUT TO:
INT. WAITING HALL – POWER RETURNS

[00:30:30]

Lights BLAST back on.

A slideshow auto-plays.

Photos include:

The deceased sleeping

The deceased blinking mid-photo

One photo of a random stranger

MAYA
Who’s that?

DEV
Wrong life.

CUT TO:
INT. CREMATION ROOM – FINAL

[00:33:30]

The machine starts.

Low HUM.

Dev watches.

DEV
(softly)
You were never good at staying.

CUT TO:
EXT. CREMATORIUM – AFTERNOON

[00:37:00]

Ash urn handed over.

Rita hands Dev a bill.

RITA
Would you like feedback form?

DEV
On my father?

RITA
On us.

CUT TO:
EXT. PARKING LOT – CONTINUOUS

[00:40:00]

Rain stops.

Sunlight breaks through clouds — awkwardly cheerful.

Maya looks at Dev.

MAYA
Coffee?

DEV
Sure. It’s not like today can get worse.

A bird IMMEDIATELY poops on his shoulder.

They both laugh.

FINAL SHOT:
EXT. COFFEE SHOP – EVENING

[00:45:30]

They sit quietly.

Dev stirs coffee.

DEV (V.O.)
Grief isn’t loud.
It just trips you when you’re trying to stand.

They sip.

Silence.

FADE TO BLACK.
END.
